Adoption Questions

What is the adoption process?

The adoption process typically involves:

  1. Browsing our available pets on the adoption page
  2. Filling out an adoption application form
  3. Our team reviews your application and contacts you with questions
  4. You schedule a time to meet the pet
  5. If everything goes well, we complete the paperwork and finalize the adoption

The entire process typically takes 5-7 business days.

How much does it cost to adopt a pet?

Adoption fees help cover the cost of medical care, vaccination, and rehabilitation. Fees vary by animal type:

  • Dogs: R800 - R1,200
  • Cats: R400 - R700
  • Rabbits and small pets: R200 - R400

All adopted pets come with medical records and vaccination certificates. For questions about specific animals, please contact us.

Can I return a pet if it doesn't work out?

We understand that sometimes circumstances change. If you can no longer care for your adopted pet, please contact us as soon as possible. We offer a 30-day trial period where you can return the pet without any questions asked. After 30 days, we may charge a re-adoption processing fee.

Do the animals come with medical records?

Yes! All our adopted pets have been:

  • Fully examined by a veterinarian
  • Vaccinated against common diseases
  • Treated for parasites
  • Microchipped for identification

You will receive complete medical records and vaccination certificates upon adoption.

What if I have allergies?

We recommend spending time with the specific pet you're interested in before committing to adoption. If you have allergies, please discuss this during your application and scheduling process so we can help match you with a suitable pet.

Can I adopt a pet for someone else as a gift?

We require that the person who will be caring for the pet complete the adoption application. The pet's primary caregiver must meet with and approve the animal. This ensures the best match and prevents return issues.

What age pets do you have available?

We rescue animals of all ages, from young puppies and kittens to senior pets. Senior animals make wonderful companions and often settle in faster. Browse our adoption page to see our current animals and their ages.

Pet Care Questions

What should I prepare before bringing my new pet home?

Here's a checklist to prepare for your new family member:

  • For dogs: Leash, collar, food/water bowls, dog food, bed, toys
  • For cats: Litter box, litter, food/water bowls, cat food, toys, scratching post
  • For rabbits: Hutch or enclosure, hay, pellets, water bottle, vegetables
  • Veterinary contact information and appointment for check-up
  • ID tag with your contact information
How often should I take my pet to the vet?

We recommend:

  • Young pets (under 1 year): Every 3-4 months for vaccinations and check-ups
  • Adult pets (1-7 years): At least once per year for annual check-ups
  • Senior pets (7+ years): Every 6 months for health monitoring

More frequent visits may be needed if your pet has health issues.

Should I spay or neuter my pet?

Yes! We strongly recommend spaying or neutering your pet. Benefits include:

  • Prevents unwanted litters (contributing to pet overpopulation)
  • Reduces risk of certain health issues
  • Can improve behavior and reduce aggression
  • Usually saves money on long-term healthcare costs

All our adopted animals come already spayed/neutered.

What should I feed my new pet?

We'll provide recommendations based on your specific pet. Generally:

  • Dogs: High-quality dog food appropriate for their age and size
  • Cats: Cat food (wet or dry); cats are obligate carnivores and need meat-based food
  • Rabbits: Unlimited hay, limited pellets, fresh vegetables daily

Ask our team for specific product recommendations for your pet.

How do I help my new pet adjust to its new home?

Here are tips for a smooth transition:

  • Start with a small, quiet space before allowing full house access
  • Maintain a consistent routine with feeding, play, and sleep schedules
  • Use positive reinforcement and patience during training
  • Introduce to new family members and pets gradually
  • Keep comfort items like their original bedding if available
  • Contact our team if behavioral issues arise

Getting Involved

How can I volunteer at Safe Paws?

We always need dedicated volunteers! Opportunities include:

  • Animal care and feeding
  • Cleaning and maintenance
  • Dog walking and exercise
  • Administrative support
  • Social media and marketing help

Email us at info@safepaws.example or fill out our contact form to express interest.

What is foster care?

Foster care allows you to temporarily care for an animal in your home while they await adoption. Benefits include:

  • Animals get a home-like environment during rehabilitation
  • Helps animals adjust better to family life before adoption
  • Frees up space at the center for more rescues
  • You get the reward of helping without permanent commitment

We provide food and medical supplies. Contact us to learn more.

How can I donate to Safe Paws?

We accept donations in several forms:

  • Financial donations: Provide general or targeted support (e.g., medical care, food)
  • Supply donations: Pet food, blankets, toys, cleaning supplies
  • Medical donations: If you're a veterinarian or have medical supplies
  • In-kind donations: Services like grooming, training, or professional expertise

Contact us at info@safepaws.example for details on how to donate.

Do you accept donations of supplies?

Yes! We accept donations of:

  • Pet food (dry and wet)
  • Blankets and bedding
  • Toys and chew items
  • Cleaning supplies and disinfectants
  • Medical supplies

Please contact us before donating to confirm we need specific items.

Can my business sponsor Safe Paws?

Absolutely! We offer sponsorship opportunities for businesses of all sizes. Sponsors receive:

  • Recognition on our website and social media
  • Promotional opportunities
  • Community goodwill and positive brand association
  • Tax benefits for charitable contributions

Contact us to discuss sponsorship packages.

General Questions

What are Safe Paws' operating hours?

Safe Paws is open:

  • Monday - Friday: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
  • Saturday: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM
  • Sunday: Closed

For emergencies outside these hours, please call +27 11 000 0000 for our emergency contact.

Can I visit the animals?

Yes! We welcome visits during our operating hours. You can:

  • Meet and interact with our animals
  • Learn more about specific pets
  • Ask questions about adoption
  • Tour our facilities

We recommend visiting on weekday mornings for a less crowded experience.

What happens to animals that aren't adopted?

Safe Paws is a no-kill shelter. All animals in our care receive indefinite care and support. We believe every animal deserves a loving home, but we never give up on them. Our animals are never euthanized for space or time constraints.

How can I report animal cruelty or neglect?

If you witness animal abuse or neglect, please:

  • Contact your local police or animal welfare authority
  • Call us immediately at +27 11 000 0000
  • Provide as much detail as possible: location, type of animal, and description of the situation

All reports are treated with urgency and confidentiality.

Do you have animals for specific needs (e.g., service dogs, therapy animals)?

While our focus is general pet adoption, some of our animals may be suitable for specific roles. Please contact us to discuss your specific needs, and we'll do our best to help match you with an appropriate animal.
